About Kolkata

Kolkata, often called the "City of Joy," is a mesmerizing metropolis that never rests. With a population exceeding 15 million souls, it stands as one of India's most populous and vibrant cities. The city's multicultural fabric weaves together communities from every corner of the nation and beyond, creating a rich tapestry of cultures, languages, and traditions. Whether you're in search of artistic enrichment, culinary delights, or unforgettable experiences, Kolkata is a city that continually enchants and inspires.

Explore Top Destinations

Discover our curated guide showcasing Kolkata's most captivating attractions. Experience the city's rich history, vibrant culture, and endless opportunities for exploration. Whether you are planning your first visit to the City of Joy or seeking fresh adventures in your hometown, let this guide be your key to Kolkata's finest offerings. From the iconic Howrah Bridge to the tranquil Victoria Memorial, discover the diverse experiences that make Kolkata an unparalleled destination for both travelers and locals.

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge, spanning 705 meters across the Hooghly River and over 75 years old, is a symbol of Kolkata's rich history and engineering brilliance, offering stunning views and connecting the bustling city with neighboring regions.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata is a stunning marble monument built in honor of Queen Victoria. Surrounded by lush gardens, it houses a museum showcasing art, historical artifacts, and exhibits reflecting India's colonial history.

4.6 (76,430 Reviews)

Dakshineswar

Dakshineswar, an iconic temple in Kolkata, venerates Kali. Founded by Rani Rashmoni in 1855, it blends intricate architecture with spiritual ambiance, offering serene views of the Hooghly River and a rich cultural experience.

4.7 (86,860 Reviews)

Indian Museum

The Indian Museum in Kolkata, founded in 1814, is India's oldest and largest museum. It features extensive collections of art, archaeology, and natural history, providing a deep dive into India's rich cultural heritage.

4.5 (51,244 Reviews)

Jorasanko Thakurbari

The Jorasanko Thakurbari, the ancestral home of the Tagore family in Kolkata, showcases rich Bengali heritage and literary legacy. It is a historic landmark, blending cultural history with the renowned Rabindranath Tagore's legacy.

4.6 (12,988 Reviews)

The National Library of India

The National Library of India, established in 1836, is a premier institution located in Kolkata. It houses over 2.2 million books, preserving India's literary heritage and offering extensive research and reading resources.

4.5 (1,731 Reviews)
